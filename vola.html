This project was a College + Amazon Adobe Creative Jam Finalist. My roles for this project were Product Design and Product Thinking.

Date

September 10 - 22, 2021

Tools

Adobe XD

The Team

2 Product Designers

The Prototype

SEE FIGMA PROTOTYPE

Problem

Lack of a filterable database with a focus on equitable opportunities.

“Inspired by Amazon’s initiative, your challenge is to design an inclusive experience in a third-party mobile app for high school students looking for opportunities to fulfill community service hours or volunteer to help their local community, no matter what their background or ability.”

From this problem statement, we identified was that high school students with accessibility needs struggle to find volunteering opportunities that match their particular skillsets.

There were some paint points in existing solutions. Scarcity of accomodating and local opportunities, as well as a lack of equitable design in existing platforms were the main issues.

Solution

Create a platform with equitable design for high school students with accessibility needs who are searching for volunteering opportunities.

We identified some initial requirements to be:

  1. Location Filter

  2. Voice Integration

  3. Accessibility Need Filters

  4. Volunteer Hour Tracking

Palette

We decided to use a monochrome colour palette with the point colour green, to try and keep things simple for colourblind users.

02/03

Typography

We decided to use simple, sans-serif typography since over-formatted text can be difficult for users with dyslexia and/or impaired vision.

Final Thoughts

See Adobe XD Prototype

I think this project will always hold a special place in my heart, since it’s the first UI/UX Project I ever created. I gained a lot of experience and general knowledge about the process of UX from this project, which also helped me immensely in my new position as UX Designer at Nanoleaf.
If I were to do this design again, I think I would establish a more concrete design system, including specific margins and a limit on icon sizing. Since it was my first project, I wasn’t aware how much not having these two things would make the design inconsistent. I owe a great deal to my teammate, who was very patient with me. I’m honoured that we were able to get finalist, but there are many flaws in the design that I think we could improve on if we were to do this project again.
